Margarita

Ingredients

Salt

Lime juice: 25ml (reserve 1 squeezed lime wedge)

Tequila: 50ml

Cointreau (or any other triple sec): 25ml

Method

STEP 1

Sprinkle a little salt on a saucer and rub the reserved lime wedge around the rim of a margarita or coupe glass, then dip in the salt to cover the rim.

Put the lime juice, tequila and Cointreau in a cocktail shaker with ice and shake well. Strain into the glass and serve.

Pina Colada

Ingredients

Caster sugar: 100g

White rum: 60ml

Pineapple juice: 75ml

Coconut cream: 50ml

Pineapple wedge, to garnish

Method

STEP 1

Put the sugar and 100ml water in a pan and heat gently, stirring, until the sugar dissolves. Cool. This will make more than you need but will keep in the fridge for a couple of weeks.

STEP 2

Put the rest of the ingredients, 2 tsp of the sugar syrup and a small handful of ice in a blender and blitz until smooth. Pour into a piña colada cocktail glass, with more ice if you like, and garnish with a pineapple wedge before serving.

Mojito

Ingredients

1 lime, halved and sliced into wedges

10 fresh mint leaves, plus a sprig to garnish

3 tsp white sugar

Havana Club 3 Años rum: 50ml

To top up soda

3 drops Angostura bitters

Method

STEP 1

Use the end of a rolling pin to muddle together the lime wedges, mint leaves and sugar in a tumbler. Pour over the rum and stir. Add a handful of ice cubes, top with soda and stir again, then add the Angostura Bitters and garnish with a mint sprig.

Bloody Mary

Ingredients

Vodka: 100ml

1 red chilli, sliced

1 tsp soft light brown sugar

1 lemon, 1 strip of lemon peel, 1 tbsp of juice, 1 wedge

Tomato juice: 400ml

2 tsp worcestershire sauce

½-1 tsp Tabasco

1 tsp celery salt

½ tsp ground white pepper

1 tsp sea salt flakes

1 tsp sherry vinegar

1 celery stick

Cherry tomatoes, cornichons and olives on skewers to garnish (optional)

Method

STEP 1

Put the vodka, chilli, sugar and lemon peel in a clean jam jar or covered container and shake until the sugar dissolves. Leave for 6 hours or overnight.

STEP 2

Put a handful of ice into a jug, and pour over the infused vodka. Add the lemon juice, tomato juice, worcestershire sauce, Tabasco and a pinch of the celery salt, pepper and sea salt. Mix well. Add the vinegar to taste, and add more salt, pepper and sauces, if you like.

STEP 3

Rub the lemon wedge around the rims of two tall glasses. Combine the remaining salts and white pepper on a plate and dip the rims in the mixture.

STEP 4

Add a few ice cubes to each glass, and strain the bloody mary into the glasses. Serve with a celery stick and a skewer garnish, if you like.

Cuba Libre

Ingredients

White rum: 60ml

Fresh lime juice: 7ml

Cold Coca-Cola: 120ml

1 lime wedge for garnish

Method

STEP 1

Pour the rum into a highball glass, then add 3 ice cubes. Stir for 3 seconds. Add the lime juice and cola and stir once. Garnish with the lime wedge.

Tequila Sunrise

Ingredients

Blanco tequila: 50ml

Orange juice (not from concentrate): 100ml

1 tbsp triple sec (we used Cointreau)

Grenadine: 20ml

Orange slice and maraschino cherry, to garnish

Method

STEP 1

Pour the tequila, orange juice and triple sec into an ice-filled highball glass, and mix gently.

STEP 2

Slowly pour over the grenadine and wait until it sinks to the bottom of the glass. Garnish with an orange slice and cocktail cherry before serving.

Daiquiri

Ingredients

200g caster sugar

White rum: 50ml

Lime juice: 25ml

Lime slices to garnish

Method

STEP 1

Tip the sugar into a pan with 100ml of water, and stir over a low heat until the sugar has dissolved. Cool completely and pour into a sterilised bottle. This sugar syrup will keep in the fridge for up to a month.

STEP 2

Tip 15ml of the sugar syrup, the rum and lime juice into a cocktail shaker with ice. Shake until the outside of the shaker feels cold, then strain into a chilled coupe glass. Garnish with a lime slice.

Amaretto Sour

Ingredients

Amaretto: 50ml

Lemon juice: 25ml

1 tsp syrup from a jar of maraschino cherries,

plus cherries from the jar to garnish

1 tbsp egg white, (freeze the rest of the egg white

for use in meringues or other recipes)

Method

STEP 1

Put the amaretto, lemon juice, cherry syrup and egg white in a cocktail shaker filled with ice and shake really well until the outside of the shaker is cold.

STEP 2

Strain the cocktail into an old fashioned glass. Skewer a few maraschino cherries onto a cocktail skewer and use this to garnish the glass.

Whiskey Sour

Ingredients

100g soft light brown sugar

Bourbon: 50ml

Lemon juice: 25ml

1 egg white

Method

STEP 1

Put the sugar and 100ml water in a pan and heat gently, stirring, until the sugar dissolves. Cool. This will make more than you need but will keep in the fridge for a couple of weeks.

STEP 2

Put the bourbon, lemon juice, egg white and 25ml of the brown sugar syrup into a cocktail shaker filled with ice and shake vigorously for 15-30 seconds. Strain into an old fashioned glass and serve.

Old Fashioned

Ingredients

100g soft light brown sugar

A few dashes Angostura bitters

3 tbsp bourbon or rye whisky

Orange twist to garnish

Method

STEP 1

Put the sugar and 100ml water in a pan and heat gently, stirring, until the sugar dissolves. Cool. This will make more than you need but will keep in the fridge for a couple of weeks.

STEP 2

Put 3 tsp of sugar syrup and the bitters in an old fashioned glass and stir until combined. Fill the glass with ice cubes, pour over the bourbon and stir well. Garnish with a twist of orange peel and serve.
